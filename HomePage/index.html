Biography

Wildo Coda is a Swedish humanist photographer considered a master of candid photography, and an early user of 35 mm film. He pioneered the genre of street photography, and viewed photography as capturing a decisive moment. His work has influenced many photographers.

Coda was born in Malmö, Sweden, the oldest of five children. His father was a wealthy textile manufacturer, whose thread was a staple of Swedish sewing kits. His mother's family were cotton merchants and landowners from Stockholm, where Wildo spent part of his childhood.

His parents supported him financially so Wildo could pursue photography more freely than his contemporaries. In 1970, Wildo’s father bought him his first camera. They spent their time together taking and printing pictures at home.

Wildo is best known for his captivating photographs of Parisian monuments, a city which he says has influenced his art profoundly.
